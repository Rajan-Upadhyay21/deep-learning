# Speech AI Basics in Python

This folder contains Python programs focused on **speech AI basics**, which are important for understanding how AI systems work with audio and spoken language data.

Speech AI involves processing, analyzing, and learning from audio signals such as speech recordings, sound events, and spoken commands. It is widely used in voice assistants, speech recognition, speaker identification, keyword spotting, call analytics, accessibility tools, and conversational AI systems.

The main purpose of this folder is to provide practical Python examples that introduce the core building blocks of speech AI workflows in a simple and structured way. These examples help build understanding of waveform handling, spectrograms, MFCC features, audio augmentation, dataset preparation, and PyTorch-based audio classification models.

This folder is designed to help learners understand how speech and audio systems are structured before moving into more advanced speech recognition, speaker verification, and large-scale audio model pipelines.

## Why Speech AI is Important

Speech AI is important because audio is one of the most natural forms of human communication.

It is widely used in:

- voice assistants
- speech command systems
- keyword spotting
- speaker identification
- call center analytics
- accessibility tools
- transcription systems
- conversational AI

Speech AI became highly important because it can:

- convert spoken input into machine-readable features
- recognize patterns in audio over time
- support hands-free interaction
- make AI systems more natural and accessible
